In a recent internal memo, Asha Sharma, the new head of Xbox, revealed that the Game Pass subscription service has become "too expensive" for players, hinting at upcoming changes to its pricing structure. This revelation comes just six months after Microsoft raised the price of its top-tier Game Pass by a staggering 50%, pushing it to $30 per month. Sharma emphasized the need for a "better value equation," indicating that the current model may not be sustainable in the long run.

The Xbox Game Pass currently offers various tiers, including Ultimate, Premium, Essential, and PC Game Pass, each catering to different gaming needs and budgets. However, the inclusion of high-profile titles like Call of Duty has reportedly strained Microsoft's revenue, leading to significant price hikes. As the gaming landscape evolves, Sharma suggests that a more flexible system will be tested and implemented, although it will take time to refine.

As gamers, we must stay tuned to these developments. Will Microsoft find a balance that satisfies both its business model and the gaming community? The future of Game Pass could redefine subscription gaming as we know it.
